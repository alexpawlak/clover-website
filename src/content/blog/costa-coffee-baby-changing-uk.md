Costa Coffee is one of those places you end up in with a baby almost by accident. You needed a coffee, there was a Costa, and now you're wondering if there's a changing table. The good news is that most of them do have one, but it really does depend which Costa you've walked into.

## Yes, but it really depends which Costa you're walking into

Full-sized Costa locations with a proper sit-in dining area almost always have baby changing facilities. They're usually tucked in the accessible toilet or a combined family toilet near the back of the shop. The quality varies a bit, especially in older sites, but you'll generally find a fold-down table, a bin, and enough space to get the job done.

The newer, recently refurbished locations tend to be noticeably better. Bigger shop, cleaner facilities, sometimes a dedicated room rather than just the accessible loo.

## Costa inside a Sainsbury's, check the supermarket not the cafe

If you're in a Costa that sits inside a Sainsbury's, the baby changing facilities belong to the supermarket, not the cafe. Head for the main Sainsbury's toilets rather than asking the Costa staff. Larger Sainsbury's stores usually have a dedicated parent and baby room, so you might actually end up with more space than you'd get in a standalone Costa.

The Costa itself won't have its own changing area in this case. It shares the building but not the toilets.

## Drive-throughs and express machines, don't count on it

Drive-through Costas are a different story. If there's no sit-in space, there are almost no toilet facilities at all. The same goes for Costa Express machines in petrol stations or train stations, those are just self-serve coffee points with no toilets nearby.

If you're on a motorway or a long drive, the services themselves will have facilities, but the Costa concession inside them won't independently. Worth keeping that in mind before you commit to the drive-through queue.

## Babycino run with a baby in tow

Costa staff are pretty family-friendly in practice. Asking for a babycino, getting a high chair sorted, warming up a bottle, none of that is usually a problem. The facilities in most sit-in locations are decent enough for a quick stop. Just don't bank on it at smaller formats.
